Chef Rehab: An Immersive VR Game for Upper Limb Rehabilitation

Immersive VR-based rehabilitation game using motion sensors and gamified therapy

Rehabilitation In Progress
Turning everyday cooking tasks into an engaging path to recovery through virtual reality.

Context & Problem

Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic autoimmune disease that affects over 2.8 million people worldwide, often leading to upper limb motor impairments that limit independence in daily activities. Traditional rehabilitation can be repetitive and demotivating, resulting in poor adherence.

Project Overview

Chef Rehab addresses this challenge by transforming therapy into an engaging experience through virtual reality. This immersive game turns everyday cooking tasks into therapeutic exercises, promoting essential movements like pronation, abduction, and shoulder rotation. By combining gamification with rehabilitation principles, Chef Rehab enhances patient motivation, adherence, and functional recovery, offering a playful yet clinically meaningful solution.

Key Features

Unity Game Engine

Built using Unity for smooth, responsive gameplay with optimized performance for rehabilitation settings.

VR Gloves Integration

Real-time hand and arm tracking with VR gloves for precise biofeedback and natural interaction with virtual objects.

Adaptive Difficulty

Dynamic difficulty adjustment based on patient progress and performance metrics.

Real-time Biofeedback

Visual and audio feedback combined with VR gloves to help patients and therapists monitor and guide hand and arm movements effectively

Progress Tracking

Comprehensive analytics and rehabilitation progress tracking for patients and therapists.

Engaging Game Mechanics

A realistic cooking environment with dynamic visual and auditory feedback (colors, cooking sounds, food changes). that motivate continued therapy participation.

Impact and Goals

The primary goal of this project is to revolutionize motor rehabilitation by making it more engaging, data-driven, and effective. Traditional rehabilitation can be repetitive and demotivating for patients, leading to poor adherence and suboptimal outcomes.

By incorporating game design principles and real-time biofeedback, this therapeutic video game aims to:

  • Increase patient engagement and motivation during rehabilitation sessions
  • Provide objective, quantifiable data on patient progress
  • Enable personalized rehabilitation protocols based on individual patient needs
  • Reduce the burden on healthcare professionals while improving outcomes
  • Make rehabilitation more accessible and cost-effective
